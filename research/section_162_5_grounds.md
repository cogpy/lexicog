## Key Principles

### 1. Court Has No Discretion

**Critical Finding:** Once the grounds in Section 162(5) have been established, **a court has no discretion but to declare a director delinquent**.

> "Further, once the grounds in section 162(5) have been established, a court has no discretion but to declare a director delinquent â€“ a severe sanction for misconduct that serves as both a deterrent and protection for companies."

### 2. Grounds for Delinquency Declaration

**Section 162(5) Grounds:**

A court **must** declare a director delinquent if they:

1. **Acted in a manner that amounted to gross negligence**
2. **Wilful misconduct**
3. **Breach of trust**

...in performing their functions and duties to the company.

### 3. Fiduciary Duty Breaches

**From Supreme Court of Appeal (SCA) decision in *Msibithi Investments and Others v African Legend Investment and Others* [2025] ZASCA 61:**

> "The judgment distils the core of delinquency under section 162(5)(c)(iv): egregious breaches of fiduciary duties that are neither technical nor trivial are sanctionable."

**Key Point:** The breaches must be:
- **Egregious** (extremely bad or shocking)
- **Not technical** (not mere technicalities)
- **Not trivial** (substantial and serious)

### 4. Statutory Remedy Purpose

**Section 162 provides:**
- A crucial remedy for companies where director's fiduciary duties are breached
- Acts as both a **deterrent** and **protective mechanism**
- Once proved, court has no discretion but to order declaration

## Case Study: African Legend Investment

### Facts

In a sprawling governance dispute, the SCA declared Mr. Mashudu Ramano (Director) delinquent for seven years under Section 162(2)(a) read with Section 162(5)(c)(iv) and Section 162(6)(b).

### Grounds for Delinquency

**Based on Section 162(5)(c)(iv):** Gross negligence, wilful misconduct, or breach of trust as breaches of fiduciary duties.

### Specific Breach: Misrepresentation to the Board

**What Happened:**
- Director misrepresented to the board that OTS56's attorneys believed a letter from Glencore gave OTS56 an entitlement
- This misrepresentation influenced the board's decision-making
- Constituted breach of fiduciary duty

**Key Fiduciary Duty Breached:**
- Directors must use their best endeavors to fulfill conditions
- Must avoid conduct that could delay or adversely affect satisfaction of conditions
- Must not take action or make statements reasonably likely to be prejudicial to success of transactions
