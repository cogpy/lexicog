### 2. Citation Quality Issues

#### JR Affidavit Critical Deficiencies

**Placeholder Text (86 of 132 responses - 65.2%):**
- Contains "[Add specific response and annexure citation if required]"
- Indicates incomplete drafting requiring substantive content
- Undermines evidential weight of responses

**Missing Annexure Citations:**
- Only 46 responses (34.8%) include proper annexure references
- Fails to connect factual assertions to documentary evidence
- Weakens evidentiary foundation for counter-allegations

**Insufficient Legal References:**
- Only 3 responses (2.3%) cite relevant legislation
- Lacks statutory authority for legal positions
- Reduces persuasive force of legal arguments

#### DR Affidavit Critical Deficiencies

**Complete Absence of Annexure Citations:**
- 0% of responses include annexure references
- No documentary evidence linked to factual assertions
- Creates significant evidentiary gap

**Substantive Content Without Citations:**
- 63.9% are substantive responses (good foundation)
- However, lack of annexure citations undermines credibility
- Assertions appear unsupported despite detailed content

## Conclusion

Both affidavits require significant refinement to ensure comprehensive, sequential coverage of all AD paragraphs with proper citations and legal references. The JR affidavit requires completion of placeholder text and addition of citations, while the DR affidavit requires comprehensive annexure citation integration. Priority must be given to addressing missing AD paragraph responses, particularly in Sections 10 (delinquency application) and 13-17 (legal requirements and urgency).

The analysis reveals that while substantive content exists in many responses, the evidentiary foundation through annexure citations is critically deficient and must be remediated to ensure the affidavits meet court standards for answering affidavits.
