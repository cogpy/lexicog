## RE: THE ABUSE OF PROCESS AND THE FINAL ACT OF PUNISHMENT

### The "Main Trustee" Deception and the Subsequent Interdict

**JR 10.10** The Applicant’s founding affidavit culminates in a profound abuse of process, weaponizing this Honourable Court to finalize a campaign of punishment against myself and the Second Respondent for fulfilling our duties. The final acts in this campaign demonstrate the Applicant’s bad faith and the orchestrated nature of this entire dispute.

**JR 10.10.1** On August 11, 2025, I was asked to sign a document which I was led to believe was a procedural matter. This document, which was presented to me by my co-trustees, purported to merely confirm the Applicant’s status as the “Main Trustee.” However, the document was deceptively backdated to July 1, 2025 (Annexure **JF7**). Unaware of the ulterior motive, I signed it.

**JR 10.10.2** The true purpose of this document became clear a mere two days later. On August 13, 2025, the Applicant, acting with his co-trustee Mr. Danie Bantjies, used this newly cemented authority to include me in the urgent interdict application (Annexure **JF6**). The timing is undeniable: the document was a prerequisite for the legal attack. My “crime” was fulfilling my duty as a trustee by inquiring about the R1,035,000 debt to a deceased partner’s estate and supporting my son, the Second Respondent, a fellow beneficiary who had exposed fraud within the trust’s assets.

**JR 10.10.3** This litigation was entirely unnecessary and malicious. The Faucitt Family Trust deed grants the Applicant, as Founder, absolute power to remove and appoint trustees at will. If the Applicant genuinely believed I was mismanaging assets, he had the power to resolve the matter internally without resorting to court. His choice to pursue an urgent, ex parte application demonstrates that the goal was not to protect the trust, but to punish me and the Second Respondent through a public and financially damaging legal process.

**JR 10.10.4** The Applicant’s bad faith is further evidenced by the material non-disclosures in his founding affidavit. He failed to inform this Honourable Court of several critical facts:

   a) That Mr. Danie Bantjies, his key witness and supposed independent accountant, was in fact an undisclosed trustee of the Faucitt Family Trust.

   b) That Mr. Bantjies was, and remains, a debtor to the same trust for an amount of R18.685 million (Annexure **SF1**).

   c) That the Applicant’s founding affidavit, containing numerous falsehoods and omissions, was certified by Mr. Bantjies himself, acting as a Commissioner of Oaths, despite his profound and undisclosed conflicts of interest.

**JR 10.10.5** The truth is that this application is the culmination of a coordinated campaign of retaliation. When the Second Respondent exposed fraud that implicated the trust’s financial administrators, and when I fulfilled my duty by inquiring about a significant unpaid debt, the Applicant and his conflicted co-trustee, Mr. Bantjies, weaponized the trust, the companies, the banks, and ultimately this Honourable Court to silence and punish us.
