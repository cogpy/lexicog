## 4. Legal Aspects and Citations

### 4.1 Companies Act 71 of 2008

**Section 162(2)** - Locus Standi
- Provides standing for directors and shareholders to bring applications
- Peter's basis for bringing the application

**Section 162(5)(c)(ii)** - Grossly Abused Position
- Grounds for delinquency declaration
- Peter alleges Jax and Daniel grossly abused director/member positions

**Section 162(5)(c)(iii)** - Intentional or Grossly Negligent Harm
- Requires proof of intentional or grossly negligent infliction of harm
- Counter-argument: Daniel's operations generated R34.9M annually (profitable, not harmful)

**Section 162(5)(c)(iv)** - Gross Negligence, Wilful Misconduct, Breach of Trust
- Requires proof of gross negligence, wilful misconduct, or breach of trust
- Counter-evidence: Systematic sabotage by others, not by Daniel

**Section 162(a)(iii)** - Oppressive or Unfairly Prejudicial Conduct
- Three sub-grounds:
  - Conduct oppressive or unfairly prejudicial to any member
  - Business carried on in oppressive or unfairly prejudicial manner
  - Powers exercised in oppressive or unfairly prejudicial manner
- Counter-argument: Peter's card cancellation and revenue diversion constitute oppressive conduct

**Section 42(2)(a)(ii)** - Director Shall Not Exceed Powers
- Directors must not act without or exceed their powers
- Relevant to unauthorized transfers and card cancellations

**Section 77(3)(b)** - Acquiescence in Conduct
- Addresses acquiescence in prohibited conduct
- Relevant to Bantjies's role as trustee/accountant/debtor

### 4.2 Trust Property Control Act

**Section 9(1)** - Trustee Duties
- Trustees must act with care, diligence, and skill in the interests of beneficiaries
- Critical for Bantjies conflict: Trustee + Debtor + Accountant + Controller
- Jax as co-trustee has duty to beneficiary Daniel

### 4.3 Legal Principles

**Venire Contra Factum Proprium**
- Acting contrary to one's own previous conduct
- Application: Peter cancelled cards on 7 June 2025 (one day after Daniel's cooperation), rendering documentation inaccessible, then complained about missing documentation
- Created the problem he now complains about in the interdict application

**Anton Piller Order**
- Ex parte application to prevent destruction of evidence
- Peter's application serves this purpose (AD 17.2)
- Counter-argument: Evidence already destroyed (Shopify data expunged 22 May 2025)

**Conflict of Interest**
- Bantjies occupies four conflicting roles:
  1. Trustee (fiduciary duty to beneficiaries)
  2. Debtor (R18.685M owed to trust)
  3. Accountant (professional duty)
  4. Company Controller (operational authority)
- This creates an impermissible conflict under Trust Property Control Act Section 9(1) and Companies Act fiduciary duties
- Bantjies had direct financial interest in preventing discovery of fraud Daniel reported
